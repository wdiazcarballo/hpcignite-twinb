3.1 Key Observations

  • PCIe Bottleneck: H2D transfers achieve ~13 GB/s, approximately 41% of PCIe Gen4 x16 theoretical bandwidth (31.5 GB/s), indicating realistic overhead from protocol and system latency.
  • Asymmetric PCIe: D2H bandwidth (~7 GB/s) is notably lower than H2D, consistent with PCIe read/write asymmetry observed in GPU systems.
  • On-GPU Superiority: D2D transfers achieve ~484 GB/s, exceeding H2D by 37×, highlighting the critical importance of minimizing host-device data movement in Twin-B simulation.
